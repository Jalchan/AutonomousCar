My Opinion



About the ethics of autonomous car, I can’t count the number of times I’ve heard this question posed as chin-stroking, far-seeing futurism, and it never fails to infuriate me. Bad enough that this formulation is a shallow problem masquerading as deep, but worse still is the way in which this formulation masks a deeper, more significant one.
Ethics can't be the same way as law. Sometimes we think this is right, but law doesn't give it approval. Such as, we thought we can just cross the lane when some car accidents happened to block my way. But we should know, indeed, this is not the right thing to do. Law is to give human a basic standard. Because right or wrong, it's so different to everyone, maybe too high, or too low. So before we get an actually law, autonomous cars can't drive public.

For employment, since AI happened, this concern never away from us. AI can do faster and more accurate than human. So it can't stop AI replace human someday. What we can do is to face it and find more jobs for human. Human can learn all the time. But we need to make one thing clear, we can't damage the balance of society. Not all the human can get the same education, so the educational level should be different. When the needs of engineers increase, we need to find our more work for people who don't have enough education.

In the other part, security and the accuracy of autonomous car should be the part of technology concern. I have good faith of engineers. Nothing could stop them to create.
